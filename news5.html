Info COVID-19

Alcohols in the sanitizers have not been shown to create any relevant health issues. Little alcohol is absorbed into the skin, and most products contain an emollient to reduce skin dryness. Allergic contact dermatitis and bleaching of hand hair due to alcohol are very rare adverse effects. Accidental swallowing and intoxication have been described in rare cases

FACT: The amount of alcohol-based sanitizer you use matters Apply a palmful of alcohol-based sanitizer to cover all surfaces of your hands. Rub your hand together using the right technique until they are dry.

FACT: Touching a communal bottle of alcohol-based sanitizer will not infect you Once you've sanitized your hands, you have disinfected them from any germs that may have been on the bottle. If everyone uses sanitizer in a public place such as a supermarket entrance, the risk of germs on communal items will be lower and will help keep everyone safe.
